Robert Metcalfe (1946- )

Robert Metcalfe, born in 1946 in Brooklyn, New York, embarked on a pioneering journey in computer science and engineering. Graduating from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 1969 with dual Bachelor of Science degrees, he continued his academic pursuit at Harvard University, earning a Master of Science and a Ph.D. by 1973.

Metcalfe's pivotal contributions began while at MIT's Project MAC, where he endeavored to connect the university to ARPAnet, laying the groundwork for his doctoral thesis. Despite initial rejection, his perseverance led him to Xerox PARC, refining his work on networking protocols. It was during this time that he and David Boggs conceived Ethernet, a breakthrough that revolutionized computer networking. In 1979, Metcalfe co-founded 3Com, propelling Ethernet into prominence as the standard for local area networks (LANs). His entrepreneurial spirit and technical acumen propelled 3Com to success. Despite departing in 1990, Metcalfe's impact persisted as he delved into writing, speaking engagements, and venture capitalism.

Transitioning to academia in 2011, Metcalfe shared his wealth of knowledge at The University of Texas at Austin, fostering innovation and entrepreneurship. His influence extended globally through lectures and collaborations, culminating in his return to MIT in 2022, where he continues to shape the future of computer science.
